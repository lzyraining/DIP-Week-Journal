Histogram Equalization

Written by Zhuoyu Lion Feb. 10, 2015.

Start with a picture of Einstein for picture enhancement by histogram equalization.

By analyzing the histogram of the gray level and c.d.f. of original picture, we can find a conncetion to the new picture we disired.

Here are the new enhanced picture, and its histogram .

By using the uniform distributed c.d.f. to manipulate the original picture, the picture becomes more brighter compared to the older one.

Analyzing the histogram, the dark and bright gray level are comdensed. As a result, the brightness of the enhanced picture are in low contrast in related to original one.

Quantization

Written by Zhuoyu Lion Feb. 10, 2015.

To reduce contour effect of a picture of low bits, we use visual quantization by adding noise to original picture.

Here we start with a 8-bits gray lenna picture, transforming it into 4-bits, 3-bits and 2-bits gray formats. In order to reduce the contour effect, we add uniform noise to the pictures.

4-bits gray picture and low noise added new picture.

3-bits gray picture and mid noise added new picture.

2-bits gray picture and heavy noise added new picture.

The results show that low bits gray picture needs more noise, sacrificing the spitial resolution to reduce the contour effect.

Quantizing is a critical processing.The results show less improvements in contour effect. Better quantizing methods are need to get better results.

Gaussin noise is also added to the pictures as an experiment. Results show that the contour effect is not reduced, but the brightness of original pictures are changed.

The reasons may be that the gradually changed gray level distribution of Gaussin noise can not play a big role in changing gray level of each bit compared to their neighbours.
